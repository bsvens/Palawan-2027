Verified safety facts · 2026

Palawan is safe

Concerns about Philippines safety are real for the wrong parts of the country. Here's what current 2026 travel data and government advisories actually say about Palawan.

Verified · Low Risk Destination · 2026 Sources
🛡️

Crime against tourists: Very low

El Nido and Coron rank among the safest tourist zones in the Philippines. Violent crime targeting visitors is extremely rare. Most incidents are minor scams — overpriced tours — easily avoided by booking through verified operators.

📍

ISIS concerns don't apply to Palawan

US State Dept, UK FCDO, and Australian SmartTraveller all flag Western/Central Mindanao and the Sulu Archipelago only. Palawan is a completely separate island chain, hundreds of miles away. You're not going anywhere near flagged regions.

🌊

Water and diving: operator rules matter

All reputable dive shops display valid PADI/SSI certification. Life jackets are mandatory on island-hopping boats. March is peak dry season — calm seas, excellent visibility. Box jellyfish risk is minimal (peak is June–October).

🏥

Medical: travel insurance is non-negotiable

El Nido has clinics for minor issues. Serious conditions require evacuation to Puerto Princesa (5–6 hrs) or Manila. Travel insurance with medical evacuation is essential — budget $50–80pp for 11 days. Without it you're taking a real financial risk.

🦟

Health prep · see a travel doctor

4–6 weeks before: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended. Discuss malaria prophylaxis — risk is low in El Nido tourist zone but worth the conversation. DEET at dawn/dusk. Bottled water only throughout the trip.

🛵

The real risk: skip the motorbikes

Leading cause of tourist injury in El Nido. Roads to Nacpan Beach are unpaved with loose gravel. The group should hire a private van — safer, equally fun split among 6+, and everyone stays intact.

Before you book

Book in this order

Some things sell out weeks ahead in peak season. Do these in sequence — flights and villas first.

🗓️ Step 1 — Lock flights now

  • Mar 11–24 confirmed at $844 — prices shift fast
  • All travelers on same United booking for free bag benefit
  • Confirm all passports valid 6+ months past March 2027
  • Group size flexible — the more who join, the better the math gets

🏡 Step 2 — Book villas immediately after

  • El Nido 3-bed pool villa on Airbnb/VRBO for Mar 13–19 (6 nights)
  • Coron 3-bed villa Mar 19–24 · look for generator backup
  • Prioritize: private pool, AC in all bedrooms, Starlink wifi
  • Larger groups: 4-bed properties exist and still split extremely well

✈️ Step 3 — Internal flights

  • AirSwift MNL→Lio Airport (ENI) El Nido — book ASAP, sells out weeks ahead
  • Cebu Pacific or AirAsia Coron (Busuanga/USU) → Manila for return leg
  • If ENI sells out: Puerto Princesa route + 5-hr van is the alternative

🏥 Step 4 — Health prep (8+ weeks before)

  • Travel doctor appointment for all travelers
  • Hep A, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended · discuss malaria prophylaxis
  • Travel insurance with medical evacuation — every person, no exceptions
  • Budget $50–80pp for a solid policy that covers adventure activities

🤿 Step 5 — Book activities (4–6 wks before)

  • PADI DSD: Fun Divers El Nido or Palawan Divers — instructor slots limited
  • Birthday Tour A private boat — request sunset timing specifically
  • Coron wreck dives: Discovery Divers Coron or Sea Dive Coron
  • Phimal Fast Ferry El Nido→Coron Mar 19 — book online min 5 days prior

📱 Download before departure

  • Grab — Southeast Asia's Uber, works in Manila and Puerto Princesa
  • Maps.me — offline maps for El Nido (spotty data in some areas)
  • Wise or Revolut card — far better exchange rates than airport conversion
  • Bring USD cash for El Nido — ATMs charge fees and run dry at peak season
