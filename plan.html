Verified 2026 safety facts

Palawan is safe

Safety concerns about the Philippines are real — for the wrong parts of the country. Here's what current data and government advisories say about Palawan specifically.

Verified · Low Risk · 2026 Sources
🛡️

Crime against tourists: Very low

El Nido and Coron rank among the Philippines' safest tourist zones. Violent crime against visitors is extremely rare. Most issues are minor scams — easily avoided by booking through verified operators.

📍

ISIS/terrorism doesn't apply to Palawan

US State Dept, UK FCDO, and Australian SmartTraveller flag only Western Mindanao and the Sulu Archipelago. Palawan is hundreds of miles away from any flagged zone on a completely separate island.

🌊

Water and diving: follow operator rules

All reputable dive shops display valid PADI/SSI certification. Life jackets mandatory on island-hopping boats — operators enforce this. March: calm seas, excellent visibility, minimal jellyfish risk.

🏥

Medical: get travel insurance — non-negotiable

El Nido has clinics for minor issues. Serious conditions require evacuation to Puerto Princesa. Travel insurance with medical evacuation: $50–80pp for 13 days.

🦟

Health prep before departure

Travel doctor 4–6 weeks before: Hep A, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended. DEET at dawn/dusk. Bottled water only throughout the trip. Discuss malaria prophylaxis — low risk in El Nido tourist zone.

🛵

The real risk: skip the motorbikes

Leading cause of tourist injury in El Nido. Roads to Nacpan are unpaved gravel. Hire a private van — safer, more comfortable, barely costs more split six or more ways.
