Verified 2026 safety facts

Palawan is safe

Safety concerns about the Philippines are real — for the wrong parts of the country. Here's what current data and government advisories actually say about Palawan.

Verified · Low Risk · 2026 Sources
🛡️

Crime against tourists: Very low

El Nido and Coron rank among the Philippines' safest tourist zones. Violent crime against visitors is extremely rare. Most incidents are minor scams — easily avoided by booking through verified operators.

📍

ISIS/terrorism doesn't apply to Palawan

US State Dept, UK FCDO, and Australian SmartTraveller flag only Western Mindanao and the Sulu Archipelago. Palawan is a completely separate island chain, hundreds of miles away from any flagged zone.

🌊

Water and diving: follow operator rules

All reputable dive shops display valid PADI/SSI certification. Life jackets mandatory on island-hopping boats — operators enforce this. March: calm seas, excellent visibility, minimal jellyfish risk.

🏥

Medical: travel insurance is non-negotiable

El Nido has clinics for minor issues. Serious conditions require evacuation to Puerto Princesa (5–6 hrs) or Manila. Insurance with medical evacuation cover: budget $50–80pp for 13 days.

🦟

Health prep before departure

Travel doctor 4–6 weeks before: Hep A, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended. Discuss malaria prophylaxis — risk is low in El Nido tourist zone but worth the conversation. DEET at dawn/dusk. Bottled water only.

🛵

The real risk: skip the motorbikes

Leading cause of tourist injury in El Nido. Roads to Nacpan Beach are unpaved gravel. Hire a private van for the group — safer, more comfortable, barely costs more split six or more ways.

Before you book

Book in this order

Some things sell out weeks ahead in peak season. The first two steps are time-sensitive.

🗓️ Step 1 — Lock flights now

  • Mar 11–24 at $844 — prices shift unpredictably
  • All travelers on same United booking for free bag benefit
  • Add MileagePlus numbers to reservation to activate bag waiver
  • Confirm all passports valid 6+ months past March 2027
  • Group size flexible — more people only improves cost math

🏡 Step 2 — Book villas immediately after

  • El Nido 3-bed pool villa on Airbnb/VRBO for Mar 13–19 (6 nights)
  • Coron 3-bed villa Mar 19–24 (5 nights) · generator backup + Starlink wifi
  • Prioritize: private pool, AC in all bedrooms, confirmed host response
  • Group beyond 6: 4-bed properties exist and split extremely well

✈️ Step 3 — Internal flights

  • AirSwift MNL → Lio Airport (ENI) El Nido — book ASAP, sells out fast
  • Cebu Pacific or AirAsia Coron (USU) → Manila for return
  • If ENI seats are gone: Puerto Princesa route + 5-hr van is the backup

🏥 Step 4 — Health prep (8+ weeks before)

  • Travel doctor for all travelers — Philippines vaccine recommendations
  • Hep A, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended · discuss malaria prophylaxis
  • Travel insurance with medical evacuation — every person, no exceptions
  • Budget $50–80pp for a policy covering adventure activities

🤿 Step 5 — Activities (4–6 wks before)

  • PADI DSD: Fun Divers El Nido or Palawan Divers — instructor slots limited
  • Birthday Tour A private boat — request sunset timing specifically
  • Calauit Safari: book through Klook or direct with Coron tour operators
  • Coron wreck dives: Discovery Divers or Sea Dive Coron
  • Phimal Fast Ferry El Nido → Coron Mar 19 — book online min 5 days prior

📱 Download before departure

  • Grab — Southeast Asia's Uber, works Manila and Puerto Princesa
  • Maps.me — offline maps essential for El Nido (spotty data in some areas)
  • Wise or Revolut — far better exchange rates than airport conversion
  • Bring USD cash for El Nido — ATMs charge fees and run dry at peak
